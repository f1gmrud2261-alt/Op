Asia Continent | The 7 Continents of the World Asia: The Countries and Regions of the Largest Continent ... Asia is the world's largest and most populous continent, covering approximately 30% of Earth's land area and containing nearly 60% of its population. Spanning 48-49 countries, it features extreme geography, including the highest point (Mount Everest) and lowest point (Dead Sea), along with massive diversity in climate, culture, and biodiversity. Key Facts About Asia Geography: Located primarily in the Northern Hemisphere, Asia is bordered by the Arctic, Pacific, and Indian Oceans. It is separated from Europe by the Ural Mountains and from Africa by the Suez Canal. Population: With over 4.5 billion people, it is the most populated continent. India surpassed China in 2023 as the most populous country. Regions: The continent is generally divided into Central Asia, East Asia, South Asia, Southeast Asia, and West Asia (Middle East). Extremes: Home to the Himalayas (tallest mountains), Gobi Desert, and the longest coastline. Civilizations & Religions: Birthplace of major religions (Hinduism, Buddhism, Islam, Christianity, Judaism) and ancient civilizations like Mesopotamia and the Indus River Valley. Economy: Key industries include agriculture, manufacturing, and vast natural resources, particularly oil and natural gas in the Middle East. Major Countries Russia: The largest country, spanning both Europe and Asia. China: A major power in East Asia with a massive population. India: A diverse, populous nation in South Asia Indonesia: A Southeast Asian archipelago known for its volcanoes. Japan: An island nation in East Asia known for its technological advancements. Asia's immense size creates wide variations, from Arctic conditions in Siberia to tropical climates in Southeast Asia.
